Destimulate Youtube

Dec. 2023 - Jan. 2024
This project is a google chrome extension designed to block out distracting elemenets of youtube. It holds a special place in my heart because it was one of the larger motivators that drove me into web development. I created this project in order to not make youtube as big of a distraction. I enjoyed the project so much that I ended up upgrading it in May 2024 for an engineering project at school with my friends. Go check it out - its on the website (Coming soon).
Destimulate Youtube may be very useful. However, it's very simple in terms of how it works. All it does is simply use a bit of Javascript to locate the elements that are distracting (eg thumbnails, shorts, comments) and either delete them, or hide them. There are easy ways to do both of these for those who don't have a CS background. Other than that core feature, I just added a user setting toggle that sets the script to delete or hide elements based on what the user wants. I've learned so much, from the basics of javascript and understanding HTML DOM structures to grappling with chrome extension setups and github documentation. Overall, an amazing beginner project and a useful tool to my life.

GPT Links

Mar. 2024 - Apr. 2024
One of my favorite projects to build, GPTLinks was a chrome extension that utilized webscraping and regex so that it can feed large texts of data to ChatGPT. This way, users don't need to copy and paste these chunks of text from a website so that ChatGPT can have context or analyize website content. However, this project has been discontinued with the addition of ChatGPT being able to access the web itself, which is why it isn't on chrome extensions.
I enjoyed making this project, and it severely put my resilience to the test. Unexpected obstacles popped up all over the place, such as learning how to web-scrape and filter the right text, painfully figuring out the structure and functions of ChatGPT's javascript so that I can fit my code in without breaking the chatbot, avoiding CORS policies, etc. Problem after problem ended up extending this project from 2 weeks into 2 months, and I finished on April 2024. On the way, I learned so much, from analyzing front-end code, to creating cloud functions, and the asynchronous nature of JS. It has greatly expanded my knowledge about web development, and has allowed me to take a look into backend JS coding, which I will probably expand on later.

Focused Calendar

Mar. 2024
There are 2 main parts to this project. The first part is a manual block, where the user puts in website domains ahead of time and then when they involunteraily access it later, the extension will auto-block the user from accessing the website. It's very similar to the cold turkey app or the chrome extension blocksite in that sense. The second part was to access the user's google calendar, and based on the event, will use AI to actively determine if the website is related to the event or not when the user opens a website during an event.
With the AI revolution and the use of OpenAI API, I thought I could leverage that in order to automatically block websites that aren't related to the google calendar event. However, it was impractical to dynamically block since OAuth2.0 work only on google chrome, which restricted my extension usage to people who used google chrome only (and not other chromium based browsers such as arc, firefox, and opera), and the cost of the API was too expensive. Also I needed a server or else API keys would be exposed. However, this project was still a sucuess in the sense that I was able to learn along the way, such as OAuth2.0, API, dependencies, and even some backend javascript.

Self improvement introduction

Personal Growth or Self-improvement is the process of consciously seeking to enhance one's knowledge, skills, character, and/or overall quality of life. It's the activity of identifying personal goals and aspirations, acknowledging areas of weakness or potential growth, and taking proactive steps to develop oneself. This can encompass a wide range of activities, from pursuing education and optimizing productivity to cultivating healthier habits, improving relationships, and fostering mental and emotional well-being. It's a lifelong journey that encourages people to become the best versions of themselves, continually evolving and adapting to meet the challenges and opportunities life presents. The beauty is that this activity can be done by anyone, at any time, and regardless of who they are. As long as individuals put in the effort, they will see the results - this is exactly why I love the game of personal development so much.

Whether you choose to actively work on yourself or not, a certain caliber of character is being built that shapes who you are. However, with self improvement, we can take that into our own hands Instead of passively letting the enviornment, subconsious behaviors, and the people around you determine your life, you can determine your own character, your own habits, and your own mindsets to become the person you wish to become. Especially in an enviornment where attention is the new currancy and modern innovations take advantage of pre-built psychology (eg FOMO, lust, dopamine systens) to systemically force their customers to use their products, it has become more evident and important to discipline ones self and have a clear cut purpose in mind. This is why self improvement matters so much; the lifestyle has become a fundemental cornernstone for anyone who wishes to accomplish something in life. By taking on the journey of self improvement, you can increase productivity, focus, resilience, self-awareness, adaptablitiy, compassion, empathy, and individual empowerment to whatever extent you wish. It builds character in a way that no external source can do. The ripple effects include significantly greater overall joy in life, the formation of new, healthier, and strengthened relationships, immense jumps in productivity, sharper thinking and self-awareness, and greatly improved overall health.

This does sound like a silver bullet, but it is a tough silver bullet to take. Its a continuious journey that requires commitment, discipline, and time to craft. I've been living the lifestyle of someone on self improvement for nearly 2 years and I still feel like I have so much to go and so much to learn - about my emotions, my past, my work potential. But isn't that the beauty of life, to keep discovering, adapting, and experiencing. If you're interested in starting self improvement, just pick an area you wish to be better at and just start pouring hours into getting that idealized version of that aspect into reality (eg I want to be fit, so I focus ruthlessly on exercising, diet, researching best techniques, crafting an identiy around being an athlete, etc.) or you can join self improvement communities and they can get you up to speed.

The Origins

Before discovering the world of self improvement, I was an average kid who just followed the school system and my parents - get good grades, make friends at school, have some extraciriculars that I didn't really try in because my parents forced me to do them, and play in the digital world. Although I always knew at the back of my mind that I was addicted to the internet and especially to video games, wasn't everybody? It was an ignorant existance, and up to 2019, everything felt fine. Everything felt normal.

Everyone knows what happened in 2020. I remember being in 7th grade in my 6th period class having our teacher announce to us that today was apparently the last day we'd have school for an "unknown amount of time". Following what the school told us, we went online to continue our learning, and that temptation at being at home and the first introduction to discord unlocked a pandora's box. A new parrallel of video game playing and online indulgence. I still vividly remember playing minecraft for hours at a time during school hours, laughing to memes with my online friends, and attempting to take screenshots of each other on zoom meetings. But, as the online year came and went, and others adjusted back to a regular life, I still stayed stuck in the online realm. Anytime I could get my hands on my phone, or on my computer, a video game would always be in the background. Sometimes, I'd get up at 2AM just to grind a nightly session of roblox. The amount of things I sacrificed to feed my ever-starving video game addiction was unparralleled.

The thing that made me snap out of this pigeon hole was that even my video game friends were starting to move on. They were taking classes, working at jobs, and teaching skills to younger children. It felt shameful to have done nothing but play games all summer, so shameful that I got pissed; pissed at video games, pissed at myself, pissed at the other habits like eating junk food and having youtube and discord to assist my video game addiction. I was done with games, and I wanted out.

With nothing to my name, and all the challenges ahead of me, there was only relentless determination. After weeks of experimentation and research, I realized the only way forward was to replace these addictions with healthier hobbies. I created the four cornerstone activities that would eventually become the foundation of my self improvement journey: Exercise, Meditation, Reading, and Journaling. I knew that I needed to train and maximize my physical, mental, and spiritual health if I were to reclaim my life. To integrate these four habits into my life, I created a daily routine to reconstruct my personal habits. Where I once was sluggishly waking up at 7:30am to catch the bus for school, my morning routine consisted of a 20 minute journaling session, a 10 minute meditation, a 5 minute cold shower, and a 20 minute cardio workout all before 8:30am. Afterschool, instead of playing rounds of clash royale on the bus home with my friends, I biked to the gym and spent 75 minutes weightlifting. Before I went to bed, I educated myself by reading for 45 minutes rather than hyperstimulate my brain with youtube or video games.

The first 2 months of this transformation was a daily mental battleground, where the allure of reverting to the comfort of hedonic pleasures clashed with my unwavering resolve to take back my life. In these moments, discipline and humility emerged as my guiding principles. The creation of these very tough habits required the commitment to show up even when I didn't want to, and to resist the pleasures even though I felt like it. It also forced me to acknowledge and learn from setbacks while cultivating the patience to persevere through adversity. Each small victory served as a reminder of my capacity for growth and resilience, fueling my determination to press onward despite the obstacles that lay ahead. As the days went past, I felt more and more capable of resisting the urges to waste time, and I slowly adjusted to my new identity.

After 4 grueling months filled with progress, setbacks, victories, and losses, I didn't feel the need to go back to the habitual distractions that once had complete control over me. This project was a holistic transformation of my physical, mental, and spiritual well-being, and I didn't expect my life to take such a pivotal turn. I have emerged stronger, more resilient, and deeply enriched by this experience, and it opened a whole new world of challenges for me to tackle that I never even knew existed before embarking on this journey. As I stand at the threshold of future endeavors, I carry with me the invaluable lessons gleaned from this journey, ready to confront challenges with discipline, humility, and perseverance.

Dopamine Detox
For those who don't know what a dopamine detox is, it's a challenge of temperance. The person on the challenge just goes cold turkey on their addictions for a certain span of weeks, and restricts themselves from getting that hedonic high you feel whenever you do something really fun for a certain period of time. This is done so that you get rid of the accustomed "high state" from unnaturally easy ways to get dopamine (video games, social media, junk food, YT, etc.), allowing you to enjoy the truly joyful things in life. Usually, it lasts for about 2 weeks, but doing it for a week, 4 days, or even a year is still going to benefit.
To me, this project is a recurring activity. The dopamine detox is perfect to start a season of work and productivity, and so I usually cycle it once per 3-4 months. I've done at least 6 separate detoxes, all with varying lengths and rules depending on what I want to optimize for. For example, on my first detox when I left music out, I realized music was actually being destructive towards my goals, so I banned it during my next dopamine detox cycle. In addition, I realized I needed a channel of communication, so I opened up my discord again for that purpose. However, the main goal of dopamine detoxes is to reset my mind so that I can do something very difficult with much less resistance.
This recurring project has and will continue to humble, discipline, and empower me. I've learned so much when it comes to my psyche, how I can use that to my own advantage, the nuanced relationships between external factors and intrinsic motivation and behavior, and so much more. Not only has the structure and mindset of a dopamine detox tremendously boosted my productivity, but it also just makes me a more joyful and grateful person. It's one of the best tools anyone can learn, and this project was the reason why I was able to stay motivated and get through the chasm of pain and toil when I first started out my self-improvement journey. I owe a lot to this concept.
20 fiction books in 40 days challenge
Jan. 28, 2024 - Mar. 7, 2024
This project was supposed to be 30 books in 30 days and I started this because I read one book of the hunger games to just re-read fiction and I fell in love with it - took 2 days for me to finish the series. After that fiasco, I just decided to continue reading, and made it into a fun challenge. I genuinely tried, but books are difficult to speed read without having their value being destroyed. Most of the books I read were fictional, and I did this as a fun challenge. Each fictional book was approximately 350 pages, and I spent an average of 6 hours per day dedicated to reading. Even my school work came after my reading, and although I kept my grades up, it was very tedious. Some book series I read were the trilogy "The Hunger Games", the trilogy and its fourth add-on "Legend", and the entire series "Orphan X" (9 books). I also read and finished a non-fiction book as well, but we won't count it to make the numbers look nicer.
Although it was made from an impulsive decision, I don't regret it and have surprisingly learned a good deal just binge-reading for 40 days straight. When I was young, I never enjoyed reading at all, my parents would force me to read books. However, this experience taught me that as long as you put in the time, you can enjoy anything. I've substantially improved my vocabulary, my speaking, reading, and writing capabilities, and my compassion and empathy for people. Definitely recommend reading fiction to anyone who has the bandwidth to do it, but don't get carried away like I did because with good books, it's very easy.
100 fears challenge
Apr. 2, 2024 - Present
Directly inspired by 100 days rejection therapy, the 100 fears challenge is just a broader experimental challenge where instead of the goal is to get a "no" from someone to feel rejection, the goal is to get uncomfortable and be okay being uncomfortable and doing scary things. Rejection is part of that, and although many of the tasks I do are inspired from that challenge, I've also created different challenges and put my own spin to this.
This is a work in progress and I'm just over a quarter of the way left. I expect to be finished before college apps so I can write about it but so far it's been really fun. Many of these challenges have been socially awkward, as I don't want to spend too much time on each individual challenge. Therefore, many of these challenges, are tailored to getting into socially awkward situations.
I've learned so much about fear, courage, and discipline from this challenge. Even though I have not finished yet, I've already seen a huge jump in capability to take on difficult and scary things. I've also realized how many fallacies our mind plays on us, and you can get away with much more in the social world than most people think. I've also learned to stop caring about what other people think of me, which is a very big personal accomplishment for anyone. Finally, I've learned how to actually calculate risk (because I did my research before jumping into such a difficult challenge) and it has liberated me to do so much more than if I were to not have these lessons I learned from these first-hand experiences. What a journey so far, and I expect to have even more fun in the future.
